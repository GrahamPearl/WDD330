Reputable Sources

  • Peer Reviewed Professional Journals
  • Established Texts (Longivity Foundational Concepts)
  • Company Video Sources on Professional Programming - by Recognized Authorities
  • Experts (Professional Bodies)
  • Prove / Test / Insured Knowledge
  • Inspiration (Insight), that is well documented and tracable to sources

Disreputable (iffy) Sources

  • Blog Posts / VLog Posts : Non-professionals or on topics that they are not professionals on
  • News Articles: The social media and even news journalists are strongly influenced by non-reputable sources.
  • Poorly Documented Processes, "BIG-BANG" Inspirations
  • Obscure Date/Unrepeatable Tests
  • Communication that is individualistic without proven process from self-proclaimed experts

Rules to assess Sources by

The following guidelines should be considered as academic and industrial practices

  • Citation / Scholarism: Academic writings are professionally reviewed. Citation counts and the quality of professional review indicates a level of academic reputation that a source has. Google Scholar uses this for the ranking of the publications that are available for reference. Ensure that the citation is applied on time appropriate and topic applyable content. Use highly rated professional journals and communities publications prior to lesser quality resources
  • Material Type / Longivity: Textbooks are generally acceptable content as the publisher needs to employ their own assessment standards to the quality of the sources. This can result in a source or solution being out-of-date due to the rapid uptake of technology. Keep a few 'hard'-printed copies of core knowledge areas to reference.
  • Video / PluralSight: Subscription services on a knowledge base are more reliable due to their interest in retaining clientele that are satisfied with the quality. Ensure that the content of unmediated content is considered in line with company produced content (Youtube: Amazon / Microsoft) prior to random consumer produced content (Youtube: Mr Jones)
  • Experts (Professional Bodies): A measure of the quality that a professional qualified individual or community has in life is assessable by the certification in a specific field of science. As the professional community will contribute towards maintaining their own core knowledge, rankings from other recognized professionals allows the identification of expert knowledge. Stack-Overflow applies a rating system by which solutions submitted can be uprated by others. This improves the measure of professionalism associated to that individual or community. Check always the number of votes and divide this by the time between posting on the topic
  • Prove / Test / Insured Knowledge: Test cases that allow for confidence to by self-exploration and review to confirm a source or outcome is significantly more trustworthy. If the conditions are unable to be replicated, then the process of confirmation is limited. Identify actionable items/tasks that can be performed to confirm the validity of statements.
